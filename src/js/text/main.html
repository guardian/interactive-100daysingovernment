100 days in government

David Cameron claimed recently that the government had not wasted a day since it was elected and that everything it did was guided by the “good book” (the manifesto). In many respects the manifesto has provided a reliable roadmap, but in others the government has diverted quite strikingly from what was on offer to the electorate in May.

With the government marking its first 100 days in office on Saturday, Andrew Sparrow has assembled a list of the most important 100 things it has done in those 100 days to see what they say about Cameron’s first Tory-only government.
